ABOUT THE PROJECT

The INMACOM ERASMUS+ project is fully in line with the regional priorities set by CBHE and the national development strategy of Uzbekistan to develop a new, in-demand, innovative curriculum in photonics and optical communications. It also aims to expand cooperation with industry to improve the labor market and international cooperation.

The project aims to achieve the following specific objectives, which directly address the issues listed below:

1. Development of a new Master's program in photonics and optical communications in 4 universities of Uzbekistan;

2. Enhance the capacity of 4 universities in Uzbekistan to educate in photonics and optical communications;

3. Establish modern photonics and optical communications laboratories in 4 higher education institutions in Uzbekistan;

4. Start piloting and implementing joint modules/courses in the final year of the project;

5. Strengthen university-industry collaboration to meet labor market needs.


The broader goal of the project is to create an infrastructure and system for training the next generation of engineers in photonics and optical communications. These engineers can solve local and regional ICT problems of government agencies, industry and private consumers in Uzbekistan.


WORK PACKAGES

WP1. Project management.

WP2. Detailed study of available courses on specialized engineering programs at TATU, UzMU, SSU, OFTUIT and other technical universities of Uzbekistan.

WP3. IMPROVING PRODUCTIVITY. Intensive advanced training courses are planned for Uzbek teachers. The training courses are conducted in different ways.

WP4. Development of 8 new Master's and 4 technical elective courses (90 ECTS) and requirements for the Master's thesis.

WP5. Organization of photonics and optical communication laboratories at TATU, UzMU, SSU and UBTUIT.

WP6. USE. Recognition and accreditation of 8 new Master's courses.

WP7. Training courses for engineers of various specialties are organized in the established laboratories based on the current needs of the industry.

WP8. QUALITY CONTROL. A Quality Control Team (QCT) will be formed from academic staff of the partner universities to monitor the quality of teaching and course content.

WP9. Impact and dissemination.
